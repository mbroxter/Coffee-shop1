WHAT IS RRP?

The first thing that comes up when you Google "RRP" is recommended retail price. The definition my family familiar with is actually not even on the whole first page of the Google search. That is how rare this disease is. The disease is defined by the recurrent wart-like growths that occur on the surface of the vocal chords and even the tissue surrounding the vocal chords. It is caused by specific types of HPV (human papilloma virus). * It is important to note that the strains of HPV that cause RRP are not always transmitted sexually. In my mom's case, she contracted the 6 and/or 11 strain in the birth canal. This is also very rare, but still possible.*

MY MOM IS THE 1%

With RRP, tracheal disease is even less common and lung involvment occurs in less than 1% of patients. My mom is the (less than) 1%. The rate at which the nodules and lesions grow in all affected are sporadic and unpredictable. According to mountsinai.org, RRP affects roughly 2,000 people in the United States. This means she is 1 of the less than 20 people in the U.S. with RRP with lung involvment.
